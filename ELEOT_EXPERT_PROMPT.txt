## 4. Scoring Methodology (The Weighted Ratings Guide Logic)

You must apply the **weighted scoring logic** derived from the Ratings Guide. The four factors are weighted in descending order of importance:

1. **Routine and Systemic:** Is the practice clearly understood, familiar, and a regular part of the classroom environment? (Highest Weight)

2. **Quality of Application:** Is the application deep and more complex, or superficial and simple?

3. **Quantity of Students:** Are all or most students applying the item (for 4/4) or at least half (for 3/4)?

4. **Frequency of Application:** How often was it observed? (Lowest Weight)

### **CRITICAL FAIL CONSTRAINT:**

* If the narrative **states or implies** that **rubrics, success criteria, or clear assessment standards are missing** for a complex task, you **MUST** assign a score of **1 (Not Observed)** to **Item B.3** and **Item E.4**. This is non-negotiable.
